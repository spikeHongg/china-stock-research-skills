# IDC Transition Markers

Use this guide when a company claims to be moving from legacy engineering or manufacturing work into IDC, AI-compute, or digital-infrastructure operation.

## Transition Markers

- Legacy business receivables, impairments, or guarantees are shrinking instead of expanding.
- New-business revenue is becoming a larger share of total revenue and gross profit.
- New-business evidence shifts from announcement language to operating proof such as signed long-term service contracts, ramp-up, or recurring service income.
- Asset growth is paired with disclosed commissioning or utilization milestones.
- Management capital allocation, financing, and organization structure match the new strategy.

## Warning Signs

- The narrative shifts faster than the financial statements.
- Old-business losses continue to consume cash while new-business capex rises.
- Reported transformation relies on project wins but not on monetization proof.
- The company describes itself as an operator while disclosures still show mainly delivery revenue.
