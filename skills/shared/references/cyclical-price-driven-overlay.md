# Cyclical and Price-Driven Overlay

Use this overlay when earnings are driven mainly by selling-price cycles, input-cost changes, utilization swings, and inventory turns.

## Typical Signals

- Revenue and profit swing with commodity or spot prices.
- Gross margin moves more with spread changes than with volume growth.
- Capacity additions, shutdowns, and inventory cycles matter more than product launches.

## Questions to Force

- What is the core profit driver: price, spread, utilization, mix, or cost curve position?
- Is the company at the high point or low point of the cycle?
- Are reported earnings sustainable if price or spread normalizes?
- Which external data source can validate supply, demand, and inventory claims?

## Output Reminder

Separate `cycle tailwind` from `company moat`, and separate `temporary spread gain` from `structural competitiveness`.
