# Sector Adaptation Checklist

Adapt the same research framework across sectors without changing the evidence standard.

Start with `skills/shared/references/analysis-pattern-selector.md` before choosing any sector or pattern overlay.

## Universal Questions

- What is the real revenue engine?
- What balance-sheet items hide the key risk?
- Which external source can validate management's story?
- What metric best shows monetization quality?

## IDC and Heavy-Asset Infra

- Check procurement portals, energy policy, regional resource constraints, occupancy or load ramp.
- Focus on capex, debt tenor, project commissioning, and contract execution.
- If the company is an IDC, AI-compute, or heavy-asset transition name, also load `skills/shared/references/idc-heavy-asset-transition-overlay.md`.

## Manufacturing

- Focus on utilization, customer concentration, export exposure, and inventory realism.
- If the company is export-led or OEM/ODM heavy, also load `skills/shared/references/export-manufacturing-supply-chain-overlay.md`.

## Consumption

- Focus on same-store trends, channel health, receivable risk, and brand pricing power.
- Also load `skills/shared/references/consumer-channel-brand-overlay.md` when channel inventory and brand strength are central.

## Healthcare and Pharma

- Focus on pipeline disclosure, pricing policy, reimbursement, and regulatory milestones.
- Also load `skills/shared/references/regulated-healthcare-policy-overlay.md` when policy or approval risk is central.

## Cyclicals and Materials

- Focus on price spread, inventory cycle, capex discipline, and regional supply additions.
- Also load `skills/shared/references/cyclical-price-driven-overlay.md` when earnings are spread- or price-driven.

## Output Rule

When applying a sector overlay, keep the same citation format and note which parts are sector-specific.
