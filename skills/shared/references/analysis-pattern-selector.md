# Analysis Pattern Selector

Use this selector before loading sector-specific overlays.

The 6 core skills are generic for A-share stock research. Only load extra overlays when the company clearly matches a reusable pattern.

## Common Patterns

| Pattern | When It Applies | Extra Reference |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Business-model transition | Legacy business is shrinking while a new growth engine is being built | `skills/shared/references/business-model-transition-overlay.md` |
| Project or order-driven business | Revenue depends on contracts, backlog, milestones, and customer execution | `skills/shared/references/project-order-business-overlay.md` |
| Asset-heavy ramp | Fixed assets, CIP, utilization, capex, and financing dominate the thesis | `skills/shared/references/asset-heavy-ramp-overlay.md` |
| Cyclical or price-driven business | Commodity prices, utilization, spreads, inventory cycles, and supply additions dominate earnings | `skills/shared/references/cyclical-price-driven-overlay.md` |
| Consumer channel and brand business | Brand power, same-store trends, channels, distribution, repeat purchase, and sell-through matter | `skills/shared/references/consumer-channel-brand-overlay.md` |
| Regulated healthcare or pharma | Reimbursement, procurement, approvals, product pipeline, and policy changes drive outcomes | `skills/shared/references/regulated-healthcare-policy-overlay.md` |
| Export manufacturing and supply chain | Overseas demand, customer concentration, tariffs, FX, capacity utilization, and inventory matter | `skills/shared/references/export-manufacturing-supply-chain-overlay.md` |
| Policy-sensitive industry | Regulation, subsidies, quotas, reimbursement, or licenses matter | Use the sector checklist plus the closest overlay above |

## Important Rule

- IDC is not a default assumption.
- IDC is one concrete example of a company that may match both `business-model transition` and `asset-heavy ramp`.
- Start from the generic pattern first, then load a sector example overlay only if needed.
