# Export Manufacturing and Supply-Chain Overlay

Use this overlay when overseas demand, customer concentration, tariff exposure, FX, utilization, and inventory cycles dominate the stock story.

## Typical Signals

- A large share of revenue comes from exports or overseas customers.
- OEM or ODM concentration, capacity utilization, and order visibility matter.
- Tariffs, FX moves, and trade policy can reshape profitability quickly.

## Questions to Force

- Is growth driven by end-demand, market-share gain, customer stocking, or FX translation?
- How concentrated are overseas customers and what bargaining power do they hold?
- Are inventory and receivable trends consistent with reported order strength?
- Which official source can validate export demand and trade policy change?

## Main Sources

| Source | What It Validates | URL |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General Administration of Customs | Export and import data, destination trends | http://www.customs.gov.cn/ |
| Ministry of Commerce | Trade policy, export conditions, tariff-related policy | http://www.mofcom.gov.cn/ |
| State Administration of Foreign Exchange | FX context and policy | https://www.safe.gov.cn/ |
| Company reports and announcements | Region mix, customer concentration, inventory, receivables, capacity | https://www.cninfo.com.cn/new/index |

## Output Reminder

Separate `reported export growth`, `true end-demand strength`, `customer stocking`, and `FX benefit`.
