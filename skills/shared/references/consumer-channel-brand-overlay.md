# Consumer Channel and Brand Overlay

Use this overlay when the stock depends on brand strength, channel health, same-store trends, distributor behavior, category expansion, and repeat consumption.

## Typical Signals

- Revenue depends on retail sell-through, store traffic, or distributor inventory.
- Brand positioning and pricing power matter more than project wins.
- Market-share changes can happen through channel expansion, category mix, or promotion intensity.

## Questions to Force

- Is reported revenue backed by real end demand or by channel inventory build?
- Does gross margin reflect brand power or temporary promotion changes?
- Are same-store growth, store efficiency, or distributor quality improving or deteriorating?
- Which external source can validate channel health or category trends?

## Main Sources

| Source | What It Validates | URL |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Company annual and interim reports | Channel structure, product mix, inventory, receivables, store count | https://www.cninfo.com.cn/new/index |
| National Bureau of Statistics | Retail sales and category-level consumption trends | https://www.stats.gov.cn/ |
| Ministry of Commerce | Consumption policy and circulation data | http://www.mofcom.gov.cn/ |
| Company official channels and regulated disclosures | New products, pricing changes, distributor or store updates | Use issuer official website or exchange disclosure |

## Output Reminder

Keep `sell-in`, `sell-through`, `inventory`, and `same-store` logic separate whenever possible.
