Use this guide when the company claims to compete in IDC, AI-compute infrastructure, or data-center lifecycle services.

## Value Chain Buckets

- Upstream: equipment, power, cooling, modules, engineering capability
- Midstream: design, integration, EPC, commissioning
- Asset owner / operator: self-built or self-operated data-center capacity
- Demand side: operators, cloud vendors, internet platforms, local state-backed compute demand

## Peer-Screen Logic

Do not force a peer set based only on labels such as "IDC" or "AI".

Compare peers by:

- Revenue model: one-off delivery vs recurring operation
- Asset intensity: self-owned assets vs light integration model
- Customer type: operators, cloud, government, enterprise
- Ramp metric: occupancy, cabinet loading, power-on, utilization, or service revenue mix
- Financing burden: debt-funded expansion vs lighter model

## Moat Tests

- Is the advantage really in manufacturing cost, integration speed, approvals, customer relationship, or scarce power access?
- Can the claimed moat survive operator pricing pressure or cloud self-build?
- Is the company earning integration margin, asset yield, or both?

## Output Reminder

For every competitive claim, show whether the proof comes from company disclosure, policy, procurement, or third-party validation.
